Where does all the fuel come from? Governments.

Global control of fossil-fuel production

But innovation is a phenomenon that is difficult to predict and influence. If innovation doesn’t pull us toward a renewable future by making the technology more viable, then we may resort to taxation. It turns out that the government is a huge stakeholder. They own and invest in much fossil production capacity around the world, meaning that their decisions will largely influence how we make this transition. As major stakeholders, national governments simultaneously wield enormous influence and high exposure to risks.

Their choices determine who wins and loses in the transition.

Taxation can motivate change but it may put a drag on our economies

Taxation is a concrete action that can be taken, though it may result in weighing down our eocnomies with unnecesary costs.

What is taxation

Taxation is a tax on the consumption of fossil fuel products, making it more expensive and thus increasing the competitiveness of renewable energy sources.

However, countries that are net oil consumers can see an overall benefit by taxing consumption

Oil consumers can act independently of oil producers to tax oil consumption. Countries that consume more oil than they produce could see an economic boost because increased tax revenue would more than offset lost oil revenue due to higher prices. If consumers do act alone, producers are better off joining them.
